Why Claims Get Denied

Understanding denial root causes is the first step. The most common reasons claims are denied fall into a few predictable categories:

  • Medical necessity (56%): Payers reject claims when documentation doesn't sufficiently justify the service provided.
  • Authorization issues (17%): Services rendered without prior authorization or with expired authorization.
  • Coding errors (12%): Incorrect ICD-10, CPT codes, or modifier combinations that fail CCI edits.
  • Modifier issues (10%): Missing or incorrect modifiers like 25, 59, or XU on bundled services.
  • Timely filing (5%): Claims submitted after the payer's filing deadline.

The good news? Every single one of these is detectable before the claim ever leaves your practice — if you have the right AI in place.

How AI Catches Denials Before They Happen

Modern AI billing systems like RCM AutoMed's ClaimScrubber run every claim through multiple validation engines simultaneously before submission. Here's what happens in the 38 milliseconds between you clicking submit and a claim going to the payer:

  • CCI Edit Check: The AI cross-references every CPT code combination against the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services Correct Coding Initiative edits. If CPT 99214 and 93000 are billed together without a modifier 25, it's flagged immediately.
  • ICD-10 Validation: Diagnosis codes are checked for specificity, laterality, and medical necessity alignment with the procedure codes billed.
  • Payer-Specific Rules: Each major payer has unique coverage policies layered on top of standard edits. The AI knows Aetna's rules are different from UHC's rules and applies the right logic automatically.
  • Eligibility Confirmation: Before scrubbing, the system confirms the patient's benefits are active for that service date — preventing the most basic denials entirely.

When Denials Still Happen: AI-Powered Appeals

Even with perfect pre-submission scrubbing, some claims will be denied — payers make mistakes too. When that happens, the AI shifts into appeal mode. RCM AutoMed's DenialManager categorizes every denial by CARC code, assigns a priority level based on dollar amount and appeal win probability, and generates a customized appeal letter using the exact clinical language payers respond to.

The result is a 45% appeal win rate — compared to the industry average of 25-30% for manual appeals. The difference comes down to speed and specificity. AI-generated appeals cite the exact LCD/NCD policies relevant to the denial, include the right supporting documentation checklist, and go out within hours instead of weeks.
